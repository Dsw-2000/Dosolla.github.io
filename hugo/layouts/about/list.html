SiLi Ding

I’m currently a third-year graduate student at the Beijing Film Academy, where I explore the intersection of sound, technology, and storytelling.

I’ve been immersed in the study and practice of sound for over six years, with a solid foundation in production techniques such as sound synthesis, audio signal processing, location recording, editing, foley, ADR, and multi-channel mixing. My early work centered on cinematic sound design and post-production workflows.

In recent years, I’ve become increasingly drawn to the potential of integrating emerging technologies—particularly artificial intelligence—into the creative process. I created an experimental short film using entirely AI-generated sound, and I continue to explore fields like speech synthesis, speech enhancement, and audio-visual transformation. To support this transition, I’ve been self-learning Python, SQL, and core concepts in machine learning. I’m currently focused on the supervised fine-tuning (SFT) of speech models for complex noise environments, aiming to build tools that not only function technically but also meet the aesthetic and narrative needs of real-world film production.

I hope to stand alongside researchers and creators worldwide who share a passion for sound, aiming to develop innovative tools for sound design that embody both creative flair and utility, advancing humanity's endless pursuit of sonic artistry.

This website is a living archive of my research, projects, questions, and side quests. Feel free to explore, respond, or reach out.
